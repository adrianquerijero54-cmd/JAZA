Our Land Is
Suffocating.

Dump-anywhere culture — It ends now

Improper waste disposal in Purok 2 is slowly damaging our land, polluting our water, and putting the health of every resident at risk. What seems like a small act of throwing garbage anywhere creates a bigger problem for everyone. JAZA ZeroWaste Project aims to raise awareness, encourage responsible waste disposal, and inspire the community to protect the environment.

The Ground Truth

Three Critical Threats

☣️
Soil Poisoning
Household and agricultural waste dumped in open fields destroys soil nutrients and contaminates the crops that families depend on.
🌊
Flooding & Disease
Waste clogging drainage channels causes dangerous overflow during rainy season and creates breeding grounds for dengue and leptospirosis.
🏥
Health Hazards
Open dumping spreads foul odors, smoke, and airborne pathogens — threatening children, the elderly, and every household in the neighborhood.

The Path Forward

Major Steps for a Change

01
Proper Waste Segregation
Separate your waste into biodegradable, non-biodegradable, recyclable, and hazardous materials. Proper separation makes recycling easier and prevents harmful waste from polluting the environment.
02
Use Eco-Friendly Bags
Avoid using single-use plastic bags. Instead, choose reusable, biodegradable, or cloth bags when shopping or carrying items. This helps reduce plastic waste that often ends up in landfills, rivers, and oceans.
03
Join Community Clean-Up Drives
Participating in clean-up activities helps remove waste from public areas and raises awareness about environmental responsibility. It also encourages communities to work together to maintain a cleaner and healthier environment.
04
Practice Discipline
Environmental change starts with personal responsibility. Always throw garbage in the proper bins and encourage others to do the same. Consistent discipline can help build a community that values cleanliness and sustainability.
